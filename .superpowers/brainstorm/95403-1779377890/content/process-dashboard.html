Process Dashboard — What sections should it contain?

When a client clicks into a process, what do they see? Pick the sections that matter most.

1

Live KPI Scorecards

Current month values vs target. AHT, CSAT, FCR, Attendance, Shrinkage, Adherence, Quality Score — with RAG status (Red/Amber/Green). MTD + last 3 months trend sparkline per metric.

2

Glide Path Charts

For every metric NOT meeting target: a month-by-month chart showing where you are today, the committed improvement trajectory, and the target line. Actual vs committed vs target — 3 lines. Client sees exactly when you've committed to hit target and whether you're tracking to that commitment.

3

Action Plan (per metric)

For each off-track metric: what specific actions are being taken, who owns it (analyst / process manager / branch head), due date, current status (planned / in progress / done). Linked to the glide path commitment.

4

Governance Checklist

Weekly/monthly checklist of governance activities: floor walks done, calibration sessions held, coaching completed, review meetings conducted. Each level (analyst, TL, manager, branch head) has its own checklist. Client can see what's been ticked off and what's pending.

5

Attrition & Headcount

Monthly attrition %, voluntary vs involuntary split, headcount vs sanctioned strength, open positions, average tenure. Trend chart. Reason analysis (top 3 exit reasons from exit interviews).

6

Management Commentary

Free-text narrative written by your ops/account manager each month. "What went well, what's challenging, what we're doing about it." Like a letter to the client. Client can acknowledge/reply. Creates a paper trail of commitments.

Select all that apply — these become the tabs/sections of every process dashboard.
